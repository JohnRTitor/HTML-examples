GYMNOSPERMS

Characteristics of Gymnosperms

  • No flowers; seeds are exposed.
  • Common in colder regions with snowfall.
  • Needle-like leaves; perennial or woody.
  • No differentiation into ovary, style, and stigma.
  • Wind pollinated; male gametophytes produce one functional gamete.
  • Cones with reproductive structures.
  • Seeds with endosperm for nourishment.
  • Vascular tissues for nutrient and water transport.
  • Xylem lacks vessels; phloem lacks companion cells and sieve tubes.

Examples of Gymnosperms

  1. Conifers: Includes pines, spruces, and firs. These are the most common gymnosperms, characterized by their needle-like leaves and cones.
  2. Cycads: Resembling palm trees, cycads have large, compound leaves and are primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions.
  3. Ginkgo: The Ginkgo biloba is the only living species in its group, known for its fan-shaped leaves and resistance to pollution.
  4. Gnetophytes: This diverse group includes three distinct genera: Gnetum, Ephedra, and Welwitschia, which differ widely in appearance and habitat.
