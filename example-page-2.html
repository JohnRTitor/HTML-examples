GYMNOSPERMS

Characteristics of Gymnosperms

  • No flowers; seeds are exposed.
  • Common in colder regions with snowfall.
  • Needle-like leaves; perennial or woody.
  • No differentiation into ovary, style, and stigma.
  • Wind pollinated; male gametophytes produce one functional gamete.
  • Cones with reproductive structures.
  • Seeds with endosperm for nourishment.
  • Vascular tissues for nutrient and water transport.
  • Xylem lacks vessels; phloem lacks companion cells and sieve tubes.
